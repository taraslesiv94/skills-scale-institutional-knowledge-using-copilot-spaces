# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA / Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA and Testing Leads own the quality strategy, test planning, and acceptance validation. They collaborate with developers and product teams to ensure features meet acceptance criteria and maintain product reliability.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain test strategies and test plans
- Define quality gates and acceptance criteria with product and engineering
- Execute and automate test suites (unit, integration, end-to-end)
- Identify and triage defects, manage regression risk
- Validate releases against success metrics before production deployment
- Contribute to Definition of Done and quality standards

### Goals
- Ensure high product quality and reliability
- Reduce escape defects to production
- Accelerate testing velocity through automation
- Maintain clear traceability between requirements and test coverage

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and acceptance criteria refinement
- Test reports and quality dashboards
- Defect triage and root cause discussions

---

## Technical Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Architects design system-level solutions, evaluate technical trade-offs, and ensure scalability, security, and maintainability. They mentor the development team on technical standards and integration patterns.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical architecture and design patterns
- Evaluate technology choices and integration approaches
- Review system design for scalability, performance, and security
- Provide technical mentorship and code review leadership
- Identify technical debt and mitigation strategies
- Support capacity planning and infrastructure decisions

### Goals
- Deliver scalable, secure, and maintainable systems
- Reduce technical risk and architectural rework
- Build team capability in advanced technical practices
- Ensure alignment with organizational standards

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews and architecture decisions
- Code reviews and mentorship sessions
- Cross-team technical alignment meetings

---

## Scrum Master / Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters and Agile Coaches facilitate team processes, remove impediments, and coach teams toward continuous improvement. They enable effective collaboration and iterative delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate agile ceremonies (standups, planning, retrospectives, reviews)
- Identify and escalate team impediments
- Coach teams on agile practices and self-organization
- Track metrics (velocity, burndown) and flag trends
- Support continuous improvement and retrospective action items
- Serve as a servant leader to the team

### Goals
- Enable predictable, iterative delivery
- Foster psychological safety and team autonomy
- Reduce cycle time and improve flow
- Embed agile practices as team habits

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Retrospectives and process improvement discussions
- Coaching conversations with team members

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business requirements and technical implementation. They gather stakeholder needs, clarify requirements, and validate that solutions address business problems effectively.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct stakeholder interviews and requirements gathering
- Document business requirements and use cases
- Clarify acceptance criteria and definition of done with product and engineering
- Validate solutions against business objectives
- Identify process improvements and optimization opportunities
- Support user research and feedback loops

### Goals
- Ensure solutions solve real business problems
- Reduce rework due to unclear or misaligned requirements
- Improve stakeholder satisfaction and adoption
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder interviews and requirements workshops
- Acceptance criteria refinement and validation
- Solution walkthroughs and feedback sessions

---

## Security & Compliance Officer

### Role Summary
Security and Compliance Officers ensure projects meet security standards, regulatory requirements, and risk mitigation obligations. They advise on security architecture, vulnerability management, and compliance practices.

### Responsibilities
- Define security requirements and threat models
- Review designs and code for security risks
- Manage vulnerability assessments and penetration testing
- Ensure compliance with regulatory and organizational standards
- Coordinate incident response and security escalations
- Provide security training and awareness guidance
- Track and report on security metrics and audit readiness

### Goals
- Prevent security breaches and data exposure
- Maintain compliance with regulatory requirements
- Build security awareness across the organization
- Enable secure-by-design practices in all projects

### Typical Communication
- Security design reviews and threat modeling sessions
- Vulnerability reports and remediation tracking
- Compliance audits and incident response coordination
